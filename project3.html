Site Reliability (SRE) Transformation

Project Overview

Our organization was struggling with frequent outages and a reactive, "firefighting" operational culture. I championed the adoption of Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) principles to shift our focus from reactive incident response to proactive reliability engineering, using data to drive our decisions.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Worked with product and development teams to define meaningful Service Level Objectives (SLOs) and Service Level Indicators (SLIs) for our critical services.
  • Established "error budgets" which gave teams the autonomy to balance feature development velocity with reliability work.
  • Built a centralized observability platform using Prometheus, Grafana, and Loki to track SLOs and alert on error budget burn rates.
  • Led blameless postmortem reviews for all major incidents to identify and address systemic root causes.

Results & Achievements:

The SRE transformation had a profound impact on the engineering culture and product stability. We improved the availability of our Tier-1 services from 99.5% to 99.98% over 12 months. The introduction of error budgets empowered developers to take ownership of reliability, leading to a 60% reduction in manual operational tasks (toil) for the platform team.
